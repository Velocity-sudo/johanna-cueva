Key Technique · Minutes 1-10

🛡️ Objection Armor

Objections aren't handled at the end — they're disarmed at the beginning. These "seeds" are planted naturally in the first minutes so when you get to the price, the most common excuses have no power.

Armor 01 · First 3 minutes

🎯 "I need to talk to my husband / partner"

Disarms the objection that someone else makes decisions for her.

After the greeting and before discovery questions, naturally insert this question:

"Hey, before we start, I want to ask you something. When you decide to do something important for yourself — whether it's a course, a career change, something you know you need — are you the one who makes that decision? Or do you need to check with someone?"

When she says "yes, I make my own decisions" (which 95% will say), reinforce:

↳ Reinforce: "I love that. Tell me, why is it important to you to be the one who decides?"
💡 Why it works: She just publicly committed to being someone who makes her own decisions. If she later says "I need to ask my husband," you can gently remind her: "Earlier you told me you're the one who makes these decisions. What changed?"
Armor 02 · Discovery phase

💰 "I don't have the money" / "It's too expensive"

Disarms the price objection before she even formulates it.

When she tells you about everything she's tried (diets, books, therapies, etc.), ask:

"And all of this you've tried — diets, apps, maybe therapy, coaching... how much would you say you've invested in total? Over the last 2-3 years?"

Let her add it up mentally. Then:

"Right. And how much of that actually got you where you wanted to be? ... Exactly. Sometimes it's not about money. It's about investing in the right thing."
↳ Effect: When you reach the $1,200 price, she already knows she's spent more than that on things that didn't work. The right investment already has value in her mind.
Armor 03 · Discovery phase

⏳ "I need to think about it" / "Let me see"

Disarms procrastination and analysis paralysis.
"Hey, how long have you been feeling this way? ... A year, two, more? ... And in all that time, what's changed while you've been thinking about it?"
↳ Effect: "Thinking about it" becomes a synonym for "doing nothing." When she says "let me think" at the end, it's already been established that thinking doesn't solve anything.
Armor 04 · Discovery phase

📅 "It's not a good time" / "Not right now"

Disarms the timing objection.
"Tell me — have you ever felt like you were going to start something for yourself and then postponed it? ... And what happened? ... Exactly. There's never a perfect time. The perfect time is when you decide enough is enough."
↳ Effect: She realizes waiting is her pattern. When she says "not now," you can reflect: "Remember what you told me about all the times you put it off?"
Armor 05 · Personal story phase

🤔 "I don't know if this will work for me"

Disarms distrust in the process.
"I also thought nothing would work. I'd tried everything. But the difference was that for the first time, someone saw me as a whole person — not just my diet or just my mind. Everything together. And that's when everything changed."
↳ Effect: Your story is your proof. She can't argue against real results you lived. And the holistic approach justifies why nothing before worked.
🛡️

Armor Rule: These seeds must feel like a natural part of the conversation, never like a sales technique. Just ask genuine questions. If planted well, by the time you reach the price, she's already shut down her own excuses.

Phase 01 of 06

Connection & Control

⏱ First 2-3 minutes

The first 7-12 seconds define whether she drops her guard or puts up a wall. Don't be hyper-enthusiastic. Be warm, real, and take control of the structure.

Opening Script:

"Hi [Name], so great to connect with you. Before we start, let me tell you how this call is going to go so you feel comfortable.

The first part is super simple — I just want to listen to you. Understand where you are in your life, what you're feeling, what brought you here.

And at the end, if I feel I can help you and you feel this resonates with you, I'll share what working together looks like. Sound good?"
🔑

NEPQ Technique: By asking permission to lead the call ("Sound good?"), she says "yes" and automatically gives you control. This prevents the conversation from going off on tangents.

🛡️

ARMOR — Immediately after, insert the decision-maker question: "Hey, and before we dive in — when you decide something important for yourself, are you the one who makes that decision?" (See Armor section above).

Phase 02 of 06

Emotional Discovery

⏱ 10-15 minutes (the MOST important phase)

Here you just listen. Ask questions, leave silence, and let her realize the magnitude of her situation. The bigger the gap between "where I am" and "where I want to be," the cheaper the solution feels.

Situation Questions:

"Tell me, what motivated you to be on this call today?"

"What are you currently doing to take care of yourself? Whether it's exercise, nutrition, your mental space..."

"What does a normal day look like for you?"
Depth Questions (the pain):

"How long have you been feeling this way?"

"How is this affecting your relationship? Your energy? Your patience with the people you love?"

"If you asked the [Name] from 5 years ago whether this is the life she dreamed of... what would she say?"
Consequence Questions (urgency):

"And if nothing changes... if you stay the same for the next 6 months... what does your life look like?"

"How much is it costing you to stay where you are? Not in money — in your life, your energy, your peace."
⚡

Golden rule: After every deep question, stay silent. Don't rescue the uncomfortable silence. That's where she connects with her pain and her truth.

Phase 03 of 06

Presenting the Solution

⏱ 2-3 minutes max

Sell the vacation, not the flight. Don't talk about modules, PDFs, or session hours. Paint the result. Use exactly 3 pillars.

Transition to pitch:

"Listening to you, I realize you need 3 things to come back to yourself:

1. Nutrition that heals. Not another diet that stresses you out. But learning how to nourish yourself so your body recovers its energy, your hormones regulate, and you wake up feeling like you.

2. Mindset and boundaries. Working through that guilt you carry. Learning to set boundaries without feeling selfish. Reconnecting with who you are.

3. Movement and structure. Not to look a certain way — but to regulate yourself emotionally. And creating a sustainable life routine.

That's Return to Her. We meet once a week for 3 months. It's completely personalized. It's not a course — it's you and me, working together."
🪞

Your story is your proof. At this point, briefly share your experience: "I was exactly where you are. Seizures, hospital, almost on antidepressants. Until I decided I needed to change from the root."

Phase 04 of 06

The Price

⏱ 30 seconds + silence

Say the price once, with confidence, and then absolute silence. Don't justify. Don't repeat. Let her process.

Price script:

"The investment for 3 months of Return to Her is $1,200.

I also have the option to split it into 3 payments of $450 per month.

[SILENCE — let her respond first]"
🤫

The silence rule: Whoever speaks first after the price, loses. If you break the silence, you're communicating insecurity. If she speaks first, she's giving you her real response.

Objection Handling

If Objections Come Up

If you armored well, these will come up with much less force. But if they do, here's how to handle them:

Phase 05 of 06

Resolving Objections

⏱ As needed
Objection 01

"I don't have the money" / "It's too much"

"I understand. But earlier you told me about everything you've invested in things that didn't work. How much of that actually got you where you wanted? Sometimes it's not about money — it's about investing in the right thing. And you deserve to invest in what actually works."
Objection 02

"I need to think about it"

"Of course. But you told me yourself you've been feeling this way for [X time]. And in that time, what changed while you thought about it? Sometimes 'thinking about it' is just another way of putting it off. Do you feel this is what you need?"
Objection 03

"I need to talk to my husband"

"I understand. But at the beginning of the call you told me you're the one who makes your own decisions. What changed? ... And let me ask you: how does your husband feel seeing you this exhausted every day? If this gives you back your peace and your health, don't you think he'd support it?"
Objection 04

"It's not a good time"

"You told me earlier that you've postponed this several times already. And what happened each time? ... The perfect time doesn't exist. The right time is when you decide enough is enough. Is it enough?"
🎯

After resolving the objection, always close with: "So, shall we start this week or next?" Don't ask "what do you think?" — that reopens the indecision.

Phase 06 of 06

The Close

⏱ 2-3 minutes

If she said yes, act fast. Don't give her time to second-guess. Confirm, send the payment link, and schedule the first session IMMEDIATELY.

Closing script (if she says YES):

"Amazing! I'm so excited to do this with you.

Here's what happens now: I'm going to send you the payment link via WhatsApp. As soon as it processes, we'll schedule our first session this same week.

And I want you to know something — what you just did, saying yes to yourself, is the first step of your Return to Her. This has already begun."
Closing script (if she says NO / not now):

"I completely understand, and I respect your decision. I just ask one thing: don't stop doing the brain dump we talked about. Write it all down. And when you're ready, I'll be here.

I'll send you a message in a week to see how you're doing. Sound good?"
🔥

Mandatory follow-up: If she didn't close on the call, send a value message in 48-72 hours. Don't sell — ask how she is. "Hey [Name], just wanted to check in on how you're doing. I'm here if you need anything."

Tone Guide

How to Speak on the Call

✅ DO

✓
Speak slowly. Calm, confident tone, like you have all the time in the world.
✓
Use her name. Repeat her name at least 3-4 times during the call.
✓
Validate before asking. "That makes total sense…" → then ask.
✓
Share your story. Not as a monologue — as a mirror. "I lived exactly that."
✓
Close with options, not open questions. "Shall we start this week or next?"

❌ DON'T

✗
Don't be hyper-enthusiastic or force energy. Be real.
✗
Don't talk more than her during discovery. Rule: 80% her, 20% you.
✗
Don't justify the price. Say it once and be quiet.
✗
Don't use coaching jargon: "manifest," "align," "high vibes." Talk normal.
✗
Don't try to be her therapist. You're her mentor and guide, not her psychologist.
